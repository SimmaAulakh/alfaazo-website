# 10 Essential Punjabi Phrases Every Beginner Should Know

Learning a new language can feel overwhelming, but the best way to start is with phrases you'll actually use. Whether you're reconnecting with your heritage or exploring Punjabi for the first time, these ten phrases will give you a solid foundation for everyday conversations.

## 1. ਸਤ ਸ੍ਰੀ ਅਕਾਲ (Sat Sri Akaal)

This is the most common Punjabi greeting, meaning "God is the ultimate truth." You'll hear it everywhere — from family gatherings to shops in Punjab. It works for hello and goodbye.

## 2. ਕਿਦਾਂ? (Kidaan?)

The casual "How are you?" among friends. Think of it as the Punjabi equivalent of "What's up?" — warm, informal, and instantly makes you sound like a local.

## 3. ਮੈਂ ਠੀਕ ਹਾਂ (Main theek haan)

"I'm fine" — the standard response to "Kidaan?" Pair it with a smile and you're already having your first Punjabi conversation.

## 4. ਤੁਹਾਡਾ ਨਾਮ ਕੀ ਹੈ? (Tuhaada naam ki hai?)

"What is your name?" — essential for introductions. In Punjabi culture, asking someone's name is the first step to building a connection.

## 5. ਮੈਨੂੰ ਪੰਜਾਬੀ ਨਹੀਂ ਆਉਂਦੀ (Mainu Punjabi nahi aundi)

"I don't know Punjabi" — ironically one of the most useful phrases when you're just starting out. People will appreciate your honesty and often switch to helping you learn.

## 6. ਇਹ ਕਿੰਨੇ ਦਾ ਹੈ? (Ih kinne da hai?)

"How much does this cost?" — absolutely essential for markets and street food stalls. Bargaining is an art form in Punjab, and this phrase is your opening move.

## 7. ਬਹੁਤ ਵਧੀਆ! (Bahut vadiya!)

"Very good!" or "Excellent!" — use this to express appreciation for food, music, or anything that impresses you. Punjabis love enthusiasm.

## 8. ਮੈਨੂੰ ਭੁੱਖ ਲੱਗੀ ਹੈ (Mainu bhukh laggi hai)

"I'm hungry" — perhaps the most important phrase in a culture famous for its food. Say this at any Punjabi household and watch the magic happen.

## 9. ਸ਼ੁਕਰੀਆ (Shukriya)

"Thank you" — borrowed from Persian and used across Punjabi, Urdu, and Hindi. A universal expression of gratitude that transcends language boundaries.

## 10. ਫਿਰ ਮਿਲਾਂਗੇ (Phir milaange)

"We'll meet again" — the perfect goodbye. It carries warmth and the promise of future connection, which is very much the Punjabi way.
